HEXIS

Client: CueMed

Cuemed is a healthcare startup that aims to improve medical adherence for people with chronic conditions.

Duration : 3 - 6 months

Process: User Research ( User Surveys, Focus Groups ), Strategy ( Finalizing product design direction), Usability Testing

My Role: I was the lead UX designer for this healthcare start-up and oversaw all aspects of UX development.

Tools : Google docs, Typeform, Google Slides, Post-its.

Challenge


CueMed is a digital health hardware startup focused on improving medication adherence and health outcomes for individuals with chronic conditions. The objective of this project is to find the best hardware solution that fits our users' needs.
Some of the challenges I faced in this project are:

  • No prior healthcare related knowledge.
  • Difficulty finding and recruiting participants who are taking medications on a daily basis.
  • Getting access to senior participants who live in care centers.

User Research


During the research phase I explored the following:

  • Understanding how the prototypes fit the user needs.
  • Identifying usability issues.

Project Goals:

  • Help guide patients with chronic conditions by managing their medications and measuring their progress.
  • Identify the most functional design for CueMed's medication management system by conducting user research and usability study.

User Surveys:

Ideated and created multiple surveys for focus group studies. Facilitated focus group studies and followed up with additional surveys to clarify design decisions. Processed and organized survey results to help arrive at product insights.

User Survey Takeaways: (74 responses)

  • 47.3% take medicine daily
  • 41.9% take medicine multiple times per day.
  • 84.3% have missed taking the medication in the past month.
  • 71.2% of the users were willing to become more consistent.
  • 70.3% said they need help with managing medication.

Focus Group Study: (2 sessions)

Focus group study allowed the participants to openly share opinions. This helped me to extract meaningful insights and opinions. The purpose of the focus group was not to arrive at a consensus or agreement on the prototypes. Instead, it helped me to identify and understand customer perceptions of the product.

How participants currently store their medications

I asked particpants to bring a picture of how they store their medications to understand their current way of storing medications and the location of where they store it.

Affinity Mapping

With the help of affinity mapping I was able to get a sense of all the information (mixed data) such as user opinions, needs, insights and design issues.

Persona

Based on the research I was able to identify the different user types.

Design Recommendations

  • As most of the participants currently use and prefer the pill organizer model, focusing on that design would be ideal.
  • Most of the participants have trouble taking medications, due to their varied schedule, busy lifestyle and trouble remembering. We can help them form a routine by reminding and allowing them to customize the settings.
  • Typically users take medications at home. They also bring their medications to work. Devices supporting both the use cases will be ideal.

Product Evolution


I closely worked with the product team and arrived at the given solution above. HEXIS is a smart medication management device. It helps patients take medications on time.


Next Steps

  • Conduct usability study to identify the following:
    • If the users were able to use the device with ease.
    • If the users were able to complete the specified tasks successfully.
    • Identify how long it takes for the users to complete the task.
    • Identify how satisfied the users are with the product.
  • Create a mobile app to accompany the product.
